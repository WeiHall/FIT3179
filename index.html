What Creates Success in the Premier League?

Demographic of Players

The choropleth map demonstrates the overall diversity of the Premier League, with every continent having some degree of representation. As expected, many of the players come from the United Kingdom, where the Premier League is run. There are also many players from surrounding footballing nations in Europe, such as Spain and France. Whilst Europe has the greatest representation, South American countries such as Brazil and Argentina are also reasonably involved, illustrating the high diversity amongst countries.

Differences Between Teams

All 20 teams within the 2020 Premier League season are run by
different owners, each with their own strategies as to how they
manage their players to achieve the greatest amount of success.
The team with the greatest amount of success (as judged by
win-rate) is Manchester City, being the only team to win more
than 60% of its games. The age distribution amongst clubs show
that most clubs have players of roughly the same age, with many
of them having a median age in the 24-25 age range. This suggests
that the age range of a team is not necessarily a key differentiating
factor between teams, as most teams are very similar in this
regard. The position distribution for most clubs also remains
relatively constant amongst each other, with almost all clubs
investing primarily into midfielders and defenders.

How Does Offence Affect Win-Rate?

The most successful offensive team is Manchester City, with the least successful being Aston Villa. The key goalscorers of the former are mostly highly experienced and prolific forwards, whereas the latter has much less experienced goalscorers coming from different positions. This suggests that investment into key forward players is crucial for success.

How Does Defence Affect Win-Rate?

The most successful defensive team is Liverpool, with the least successful being Aston Villa. The main defensive contributions of the former surprisingly arise from mostly non-defensive positions, whereas the latter has almost all of their contributions from exclusively defensive players. This suggests that having offensively positioned players that can contribute defensively may create significant benefit for a club.
